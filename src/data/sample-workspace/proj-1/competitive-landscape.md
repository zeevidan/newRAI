## 1. Purpose

This memo summarizes competitive positioning ahead of the FY2027 strategy offsite. It is intended to inform agent-assisted research workflows and the executive summary currently in draft.

## 2. Market snapshot

Enterprise buyers are consolidating around platforms that combine **analytics**, **workflow automation**, and **governed AI**. Our window is narrowing as incumbents ship copilot features, but none offer coordinated multi-agent teams with approval gates.

| Competitor | FY2026 est. revenue | Primary wedge | Weakness |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Northstar Analytics | $840M | Reporting & BI | No native agent orchestration |
| Meridian Suite | $1.2B | Integrations catalog | 6–9 mo implementation cycles |
| Apex Insights | $310M | Time-to-value | Limited enterprise audit controls |
| ClearPath AI (emerging) | $95M | Agent copilots | Single-agent only; no team playbooks |

## 3. Where we win

1. **Agent teams, not copilots** — Competitors bolt a chat box onto existing products. We ship role-based agents with handoffs, manager escalation, and task ownership.
2. **Research-to-deliverable loop** — Sources, synthesis, stakeholder review, and export live in one workspace instead of five tools.
3. **Governance by default** — Vault-backed secrets, manual approval for external actions, and full activity logs satisfy security reviews Apex and ClearPath cannot pass today.

## 4. Risks

- **Meridian partnership rumor:** If they acquire a workflow vendor in Q3, our integration story weakens.
- **Price compression:** Apex undercut us by 18% in two recent healthcare deals.
- **Buyer fatigue:** Three of six recent RFPs cited "too many AI pilots" as a blocker.
